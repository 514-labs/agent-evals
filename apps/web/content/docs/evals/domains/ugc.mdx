# UGC (User-Generated Content)

UGC domains combine social content events with moderation and trust signals, typically across semi-structured data models.

## Core Data

- Posts, comments, reactions, and edit histories.
- Moderation signals (flags, policy labels, reviewer actions).
- Content metadata and embedding or tagging attributes.
- Creator, consumer, and community dimensions for behavior analysis.

## Characteristic Challenges

- Variable schema payloads are often JSON-heavy and evolve quickly.
- Content and moderation systems produce mixed structured and unstructured fields.
- Search and analytics requirements must coexist over the same corpus.
- High write rates from interaction streams can create skewed partitions.

## Evaluation Focus

- Normalize flexible JSON content while preserving important raw attributes.
- Build models that connect engagement outcomes with moderation events.
- Support both analytical queries and search-oriented indexing needs.
- Design robust pipelines for policy, safety, and trust reporting.

## Typical Outputs

- Engagement metrics by content type, community, and creator cohort.
- Moderation turnaround and enforcement effectiveness dashboards.
- Flag-to-action conversion funnels and false-positive monitoring.
- Search-ready content summary tables with analytical rollups.
