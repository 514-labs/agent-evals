description: "Data warehousing patterns for orders, inventory, and customer behavior analytics."
---

E-commerce workloads depend on correct transactional modeling across orders, catalog, inventory, and customer journeys.

## Core Data

- Order headers, order line items, and payment records.
- Inventory snapshots, stock movements, and fulfillment updates.
- Product catalog attributes, pricing history, and merchandising metadata.
- Customer behavior events tied to sessions, carts, and purchases.

## Characteristic Challenges

- Transactional correctness is critical for revenue and margin reporting.
- Multi-table joins are complex across order, payment, shipment, and return flows.
- Slowly changing catalog and pricing dimensions affect historical consistency.
- Late-arriving fulfillment or return events can alter previously computed metrics.

## Evaluation Focus

- Model fact and dimension tables that preserve financial correctness.
- Reconcile order and payment states across asynchronous systems.
- Build performant joins for funnel, inventory, and margin analysis.
- Implement backfill-safe logic for corrections and restatements.

## Typical Outputs

- Gross merchandise value and net revenue by channel.
- Cart-to-checkout funnel conversion by segment.
- Inventory turnover and stockout risk dashboards.
- Return-rate and fulfillment latency analytics by category.
