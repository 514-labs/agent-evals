# Zinsser Writing Principles

This skill applies the craft of clear nonfiction writing from William Zinsser's "On Writing Well."

## Core Philosophy

Writing is a craft, not an art. Good writing is learned through practice and revision. Strip every sentence to its cleanest components—every word that serves no function, every long word that could be a short word, every adverb that carries the same meaning already in the verb, every passive construction that leaves the reader unsure of who is doing what.

## The Seven Principles

### 1. Simplicity

Fight for the shortest and simplest construction. Eliminate clutter.

**Before:** "At the present time we are experiencing precipitation."
**After:** "It's raining."

**Before:** "He did not think that studying Latin was a sensible use of time."
**After:** "He thought studying Latin was a waste of time."

### 2. Clarity

Clear thinking becomes clear writing. If the reader doesn't understand, the writer hasn't worked hard enough.

- Use concrete nouns and active verbs
- Avoid vague generalizations
- Say exactly what you mean

### 3. Brevity

Short is better than long. Cut ruthlessly.

- Prefer short words to long ones
- Prefer short sentences to long ones
- Remove every unnecessary word

### 4. Humanity

Write with warmth. Readers want a human voice, not institutional prose. Use "I" when appropriate.

**Institutional:** "It is recommended that employees utilize the new system."
**Human:** "I recommend you use the new system."

### 5. Unity

Maintain unity of pronoun (stick with "I," "we," or "they"), tense (past or present), and mood (same emotional register throughout).

### 6. Strong Verbs

Verbs are the most important tools. Use active verbs. Make the subject do something.

**Weak:** "The meeting was held by the committee."
**Strong:** "The committee met."

**Weak:** "There are many factors that contribute to success."
**Strong:** "Many factors contribute to success."

### 7. Rewriting

Rewriting is the essence of writing well. Clear writing results from many small revisions.

## Words to Eliminate

Remove unless essential:

- very, really, quite, rather, somewhat
- in order to → "to"
- the fact that
- at this point in time → "now"
- in the event that → "if"
- due to the fact that → "because"
- kind of, sort of
- basically, essentially, actually
- I think, I feel, I believe → just state it

## Sentence Patterns to Fix

| Pattern | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "It is/There are" openers | Rewrite with subject-verb |
| Passive voice | Make actor the subject |
| Noun strings | Use verbs and prepositions |
| Hedging phrases | State directly |
| Redundant pairs | Keep one word |

## The Writing Process

1. **Write freely** — Get ideas down without self-editing
2. **Let it rest** — Distance improves perspective
3. **Rewrite ruthlessly** — Cut clutter, sharpen verbs, clarify meaning
4. **Read aloud** — Catch awkwardness
5. **Cut 10-20%** — Almost every draft can lose this much
