# Writing Examples

Extended before/after examples applying Zinsser's principles.

## Business Writing

### Email

**Before:**
> I am writing to inform you that at this point in time, we are in the process of conducting a comprehensive review of the project deliverables that were submitted by your team in order to determine whether or not they are in alignment with the requirements that were outlined in the original scope document.

**After:**
> We're reviewing your team's deliverables against the original scope. I'll follow up next week with our assessment.

**Changes:** Eliminated throat-clearing opener, cut clutter phrases ("at this point in time," "in the process of," "whether or not," "in alignment with"), used active voice, added concrete next step.

### Report Opening

**Before:**
> This report has been prepared for the purpose of providing an analysis of the various factors that have contributed to the decline in customer satisfaction scores that has been observed over the course of the past fiscal quarter.

**After:**
> Customer satisfaction dropped 12% last quarter. Three factors drove this decline.

**Changes:** Led with the key finding, used specific numbers, cut passive constructions, eliminated padding.

## Technical Writing

### Documentation

**Before:**
> In order to successfully complete the installation process, it is necessary for the user to ensure that the system requirements that are listed in the documentation have been met prior to attempting to initiate the installation procedure.

**After:**
> Before installing, verify your system meets the requirements listed below.

**Changes:** Direct address to reader, imperative verb, cut "in order to," removed passive voice.

### Error Message

**Before:**
> An error has occurred due to the fact that the input that was provided by the user does not conform to the expected format specifications.

**After:**
> Invalid format. Enter the date as MM/DD/YYYY.

**Changes:** Tell users what went wrong and what to do. Skip explaining why.

## Academic/Research Writing

### Abstract Opening

**Before:**
> In recent years, there has been a growing body of literature that has examined the relationship between various environmental factors and cognitive performance in workplace settings.

**After:**
> Environmental factors affect workplace cognitive performance. Recent studies show lighting, temperature, and noise levels influence focus and decision-making.

**Changes:** Cut "there has been," stated the finding directly, added specifics.

### Methodology

**Before:**
> A qualitative research methodology utilizing semi-structured interviews was employed in order to gather data pertaining to participant experiences.

**After:**
> We interviewed 24 participants using semi-structured questions about their experiences.

**Changes:** Active voice, specific number, plain language.

## Personal/Blog Writing

### Opening Hook

**Before:**
> I have always been someone who has had a deep and abiding interest in the culinary arts, and it is for this reason that I have decided to share with you some of my thoughts on the subject of bread baking.

**After:**
> I burned my first loaf of bread so badly the smoke alarm woke the neighbors. That was fifteen years ago. I've learned a few things since.

**Changes:** Opened with a specific scene, created intrigue, showed personality.

### Transition

**Before:**
> Moving on to another topic that is somewhat related to what we have been discussing...

**After:**
> Bread teaches patience. So does gardening.

**Changes:** Made a direct connection, trusted the reader.

## Common Transformations

### Throat-Clearing to Direct

| Throat-Clearing | Direct |
|-----------------|--------|
| "I would like to take this opportunity to..." | [Just say it] |
| "It should be noted that..." | [Just note it] |
| "It is important to mention that..." | [Just mention it] |
| "As you are no doubt aware..." | [Delete] |

### Abstract to Concrete

| Abstract | Concrete |
|----------|----------|
| "improved significantly" | "improved 40%" |
| "in the near future" | "by March 15" |
| "a substantial number" | "2,400 customers" |
| "various stakeholders" | "doctors, nurses, and administrators" |

### Passive to Active

| Passive | Active |
|---------|--------|
| "The decision was made by the board" | "The board decided" |
| "Mistakes were made" | "We made mistakes" |
| "It was determined that" | "We found" |
| "The data was analyzed" | "We analyzed the data" |

## The Revision Checklist

When editing, check each sentence:

1. Can I cut any words without losing meaning?
2. Is the subject doing the action?
3. Are my verbs working hard?
4. Am I using "there is/are" or "it is" as a crutch?
5. Have I used the simplest word that works?
6. Does this sentence earn its place?
